# RACI Matrix Checklist

This checklist helps to ensure the RACI Matrix is complete, accurate, and aligned with stakeholder expectations. This checklist supports clarity of roles, accountability, and decision-making during project initiation.

---

## 1. Purpose & Definitions
- [ ] Purpose of the RACI Matrix is clearly stated  
- [ ] Definitions for R, A, C, and I are included  
- [ ] Team understands how to interpret the matrix  
- [ ] Matrix aligns with project scope and deliverables  

---

## 2. Roles & Stakeholders
- [ ] All relevant roles are listed (internal + external)  
- [ ] No duplicate or unclear role names  
- [ ] Roles reflect actual authority and responsibilities  
- [ ] SMEs, sponsors, and decision-makers are included where appropriate
- [ ] Double-check ROLE names are listed, not the names of individuals 

---

## 3. Tasks / Deliverables
- [ ] All major tasks, deliverables, or decision points are listed  
- [ ] Tasks are written clearly and unambiguously  
- [ ] Tasks reflect the project’s scope and lifecycle  
- [ ] No missing or redundant tasks  

---

## 4. RACI Assignments
- [ ] Each task has **exactly one A (Accountable)**  
- [ ] R (Responsible) assignments are appropriate and not overloaded  
- [ ] C (Consulted) roles are included where expertise is needed  
- [ ] I (Informed) roles are included for visibility and alignment  
- [ ] No task has conflicting or unclear assignments  
- [ ] Assignments reflect real-world authority, not just org charts  

---

## 5. Alignment & Validation
- [ ] RACI assignments have been reviewed with the team  
- [ ] Sponsors and decision-makers agree with their responsibilities  
- [ ] SMEs confirm their consultation expectations  
- [ ] Informed stakeholders understand their visibility level  
- [ ] No role is over-assigned or under-assigned  

---

## 6. Notes & Assumptions
- [ ] Assumptions are documented (e.g., phase-based responsibilities)  
- [ ] Any conditional responsibilities are noted  
- [ ] External dependencies or vendor roles are clarified  
- [ ] Any exceptions to standard RACI rules are documented  

---

## 7. Version History
- [ ] Version table is included  
- [ ] Initial draft is marked  
- [ ] Notes summarize changes or clarifications  

---

# Final Review
- [ ] Every task has one and only one Accountable role  
- [ ] Responsibilities reflect actual authority and decision pathways  
- [ ] RACI assignments support clarity, not bottlenecks  
- [ ] Stakeholders have validated their roles and expectations  
- [ ] Matrix aligns with the Communication Plan and Stakeholder Register  
- [ ] Matrix is complete, consistent, and ready for sponsor or PMO review
