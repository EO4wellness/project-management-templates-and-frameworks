# Project Charter Thought Guide

Below are guiding questions which can be usedful in strengthen clarity, alignment, and feasibility during the important project initiation phase. The question prompts help ensure the project charter is well grounded. 
---

## 1. Purpose & Problem Definition
- What problem are we actually solving, and who experiences it?
- What happens if we do nothing?
- How do we know this problem is real and not an assumption?
- What evidence supports the need for this project?
- Is this project solving a root cause or a symptom?

---

## 2. Business Case & Value
- What value does this project create (financial, operational, compliance, customer experience)?
- Who benefits from this value?
- How will we measure whether the value was delivered?
- Is the expected value worth the cost, time, and risk?
- What alternatives were considered, and why was this approach chosen?

---

## 3. Objectives & Success Criteria
- What does “done” look like for the sponsor?
- What does “done” look like for the team?
- What does “done” look like for the customer or end user?
- Are the objectives measurable and realistic?
- What would make this project successful in the eyes of leadership?

---

## 4. Scope & Boundaries
- What is *in* scope?
- What is *explicitly out* of scope?
- What assumptions are we making that could be wrong?
- What boundaries must be respected (legal, technical, operational)?
- What work is intentionally excluded to prevent scope creep?

---

## 5. Deliverables
- What tangible outputs will this project produce?
- Who will use each deliverable?
- What does “acceptable quality” mean for each deliverable?
- Are any deliverables dependent on external teams or vendors?

---

## 6. Assumptions & Constraints
- What conditions are we assuming to be true?
- What constraints already exist (budget, time, resources, compliance)?
- Which assumptions, if wrong, would significantly impact the project?
- Which constraints are negotiable, and which are fixed?

---

## 7. Risks & Issues (High-Level)
- What could derail this project early?
- What risks are we accepting by moving forward?
- What known issues already exist?
- What early warning signs should we watch for?
- What mitigation strategies are realistic at this stage?

---

## 8. Stakeholders (High-Level)
- Who cares if this project succeeds or fails?
- Who has the power to block or accelerate the project?
- Who will be impacted even if they aren’t directly involved?
- Who needs to be informed, consulted, or engaged early?
- Are there hidden stakeholders (support teams, compliance, operations)?

---

## 9. Milestones & Timeline
- What are the major phases or checkpoints?
- What deadlines are fixed vs. flexible?
- What dependencies could affect the timeline?
- What early milestones can validate progress?

---

## 10. Budget & Resources
- What resources are required (people, tools, vendors)?
- Is the budget realistic for the scope?
- What trade-offs might be required if constraints tighten?
- Who approves budget changes?

---

## 11. Governance & Decision-Making
- Who is the sponsor?
- What decisions can the PM make independently?
- What decisions require escalation?
- What is the escalation path?
- How will changes to scope, timeline, or budget be approved?

---

# How to Use This Guide
Use these questions:
- Before drafting the charter (to clarify thinking)
- During drafting (to fill gaps)
- Before sponsor review (to ensure completeness)
- During kickoff (to align the team)
- For AI brainstorming (if NDA-safe) 

This guide helps transform the charter from a document into a shared understanding of the project’s purpose, boundaries, and success conditions.  Use AI tools for brainstorming when appropriate and NDA‑safe; always remember to avoid entering sensitive or confidential information.
