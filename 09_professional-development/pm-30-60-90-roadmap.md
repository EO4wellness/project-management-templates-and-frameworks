# 30/60/90 PM Ramp‑Up Roadmap  
A practical onboarding guide for new Project Managers — designed to build confidence, competence, and momentum during the first three months in a PM role.

This roadmap focuses on **execution**, **communication**, and **alignment**, not theory. Use it as a structured way to grow into your responsibilities while learning the tools, workflows, and expectations of modern PM practice.

---

# 🔵 First 30 Days — Foundation & Orientation  
**Goal:** Understand the environment, tools, stakeholders, and expectations.

### 🎯 Key Focus Areas
- Learn the project landscape: scope, constraints, timelines, risks.
- Understand team roles, responsibilities, and communication norms.
- Get access to all PM tools (Jira, Asana, Smartsheet, Confluence, etc.).
- Review existing project documentation and artifacts.
- Shadow meetings to observe team dynamics and workflows.

### 📌 Actions
- Read project charters, requirements, and stakeholder registers.
- Map out who does what — create a simple RACI for your own clarity.
- Attend standups, sprint planning, retros, and stakeholder syncs.
- Review RAID logs, risk registers, and status reports.
- Identify gaps in documentation or communication.

### 📈 Outcomes
- You understand the project, the people, and the tools.
- You know how work flows through the system.
- You can speak confidently about project goals and constraints.

---

# 🟣 Days 31–60 — Execution & Ownership  
**Goal:** Begin managing components of the project and driving clarity.

### 🎯 Key Focus Areas
- Take ownership of recurring PM responsibilities.
- Improve communication flow between stakeholders and the team.
- Strengthen your understanding of Agile, hybrid, or predictive workflows.
- Start managing risks, issues, and dependencies.

### 📌 Actions
- Run standups or co‑facilitate sprint planning.
- Draft or update RAID logs, risk registers, and decision logs.
- Create or refine project documentation (SOPs, meeting notes, workflows).
- Begin sending weekly status updates.
- Identify bottlenecks and propose small process improvements.

### 📈 Outcomes
- You’re contributing directly to project execution.
- You’re trusted to manage communication and documentation.
- You’re reducing friction and improving team alignment.

---

# 🟢 Days 61–90 — Leadership & Optimization  
**Goal:** Demonstrate independent PM capability and improve project performance.

### 🎯 Key Focus Areas
- Lead ceremonies and stakeholder communication.
- Drive process improvements and workflow clarity.
- Strengthen cross‑functional collaboration.
- Begin thinking strategically about risks, timelines, and delivery.

### 📌 Actions
- Fully own sprint planning, retros, or weekly syncs.
- Build or refine workflow diagrams (Mermaid, Lucidchart, etc.).
- Implement small automation (templates, scripts, dashboards).
- Partner with leads to forecast risks and adjust plans proactively.
- Deliver a 90‑day reflection: wins, gaps, and proposed improvements.

### 📈 Outcomes
- You operate as a fully functioning PM.
- You improve processes, communication, and predictability.
- You demonstrate leadership, clarity, and initiative.

---

# 🧭 How to Use This Roadmap
- **New PMs:** Follow this as a structured onboarding plan.  
- **Career changers:** Use it to understand what PMs actually do day‑to‑day.  
- **Managers:** Use it to onboard junior PMs or PMO analysts.  

This roadmap is intentionally flexible — adapt it to your team, project, and delivery model.
