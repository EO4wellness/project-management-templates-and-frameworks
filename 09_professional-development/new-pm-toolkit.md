# New PM Toolkit  
A practical, beginner‑friendly roadmap for anyone entering Project Management — whether you’re pivoting from another field, starting your first PM role, or building skills alongside CAPM/PMP certification.

This toolkit highlights **high‑value supplemental skills** that make a new PM job‑ready beyond core PMBOK knowledge.

---

## Why This Toolkit Exists
Earning a PM certification (CAPM/PMP) builds foundational knowledge — but employers also expect familiarity with tools, workflows, communication practices, and basic technical literacy.

This guide helps you identify **what to learn next** so you can confidently step into a PM role, or be more confident in your next PM role.

---

# 1️⃣ Software Skills  
**Value:** PMs work inside tools every day. Knowing them makes you productive from day one.

**Recommended learning:**
- **Agile with Atlassian Jira**  
  Learn sprints, backlogs, workflows, and Agile fundamentals.
- **Google Project Management: Project Execution & Tools**  
  Covers Asana, Trello, Smartsheet, RACI charts, risk logs.
- **Microsoft Project**  
  Essential for Gantt charts, critical path, resource leveling.

**Best for:** Tech, SaaS, enterprise, government, healthcare, PMO roles.

---

# 2️⃣ Technical Literacy  
**Value:** PMs don’t need to code — but they *do* need to understand how systems work and communicate with engineering.

**Recommended learning:**
- **Python for Everybody**  
  Light automation, file handling, APIs — enough to read scripts and automate reports.
- **Google IT Support (selected modules only):**  
  - Bits & Bytes of Networking  
  - Operating Systems  
  - System Administration Basics  
  - IT Security Fundamentals  
- **IBM Introduction to Cloud Computing**  
  Vendor‑neutral cloud fundamentals (APIs, containers, microservices).

**Best for:** PMs in tech, cloud, AI, SaaS, or any environment with engineering teams.

---

# 3️⃣ Business & Operations Skills  
**Value:** Many PM roles blend project work with business analysis and process improvement.

**Recommended learning:**
- **Business Analysis & Process Management**  
  Requirements, workflows, stakeholder analysis.
- **Excel Skills for Business**  
  Dashboards, pivot tables, data cleaning.
- **Operations Management (Wharton)**  
  Bottlenecks, throughput, capacity planning.

**Best for:** PMO, operations, implementation, healthcare, logistics.

---

# 4️⃣ Communication & Leadership  
**Value:** PMs spend most of their time communicating — writing updates, aligning stakeholders, negotiating timelines.

**Recommended learning:**
- **High‑Impact Business Writing**  
  Clear, concise communication.
- **Successful Negotiation**  
  Conflict resolution, persuasion, stakeholder alignment.

**Best for:** Every PM, regardless of industry.

---

# 5️⃣ (Optional) Domain‑Specific PM  
**Value:** Some industries expect PMs to understand their environment before hiring.

**Recommended learning:**
- **Healthcare Project Management**  
  Clinical workflows, regulatory environments.
- **AI Product Management (Duke / DeepLearning.AI)**  
  AI lifecycle, data pipelines.
- **Construction Project Management (Columbia)**  
  Scheduling, contracts, safety.
- **Supply Chain Management (Rutgers)**  
  Logistics, inventory, operations.
- **Cybersecurity Foundations (IBM / Google)**  
  Risk, security frameworks.

**Best for:** PMs entering specialized industries or leveraging prior experience.

---

## 📌 How to Use This Toolkit
Choose the categories that match your goals:

- **Pivoting into PM?**  
  Start with Software Skills + Communication.
- **Starting your first PM role?**  
  Add Technical Literacy + Business & Operations.
- **Maintaining certification?**  
  Use these courses for continuing education.
- **Targeting a specific industry?**  
  Explore Domain‑Specific PM.

This toolkit is intentionally flexible.  There are a few pitfalls to avoid.  Study doesn't replace working knowledge. Don't let the availability of courses slow you down from working in the Project Management field.  Tools, and PM techniques become outdated. Courses do too.  Be mindful. If in doubt, ask an LLM if the course you've selected is current.
