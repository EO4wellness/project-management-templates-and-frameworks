# Agile Estimation Guide (Points vs Hours)

Agile teams estimate work to improve predictability, support planning, and align expectations. This guide explains when to use points, when to use hours, and how hybrid estimation works in mixed environments.

---

## Estimating in Story Points

Story points measure **relative complexity**, not time. They are useful when:
- Work varies widely in complexity
- Teams want to avoid anchoring on hours
- Velocity is used for planning
- Multiple roles contribute to a single item

### When Points Work Best
- Cross‑functional teams
- Mature Agile environments
- Work with unknowns or variability
- Teams practicing continuous improvement

---

## Estimating in Hours

Hours measure **actual effort**. They are useful when:
- Stakeholders require time‑based forecasting
- Work is predictable and repetitive
- Teams need capacity planning in concrete terms
- WBS‑based planning is already in place

### When Hours Work Best
- Operations, support, or maintenance work
- Hybrid Agile + traditional projects
- Teams with fixed weekly capacity
- Environments with strict deadlines

---

## Hybrid Estimation (Points + Hours)

Hybrid estimation combines the strengths of both approaches:
- Points for sprint planning and velocity
- Hours for budgeting, forecasting, and WBS alignment

### Hybrid Works Best When
- Teams use Agile but leadership needs time‑based reporting
- Work includes both creative and predictable tasks
- Projects blend iterative and waterfall components

---

## Common Estimation Pitfalls
- Confusing effort with duration
- Over‑precision (false accuracy)
- Estimating without clear acceptance criteria
- Ignoring dependencies or external approvals
- Using points as hours (they are not interchangeable)

---

## Thinking Prompts
- What level of uncertainty exists in this work?
- Does the team benefit more from relative or absolute estimation?
- Are stakeholders expecting time‑based forecasts?
- How often do estimates need to be revisited?
- Does the estimation method support predictable delivery?
