# Backlog Grooming Guide

## Goals of Grooming
- Keep the backlog prioritized
- Ensure top items are ready for development
- Remove outdated or irrelevant items
- Break down large items into smaller, actionable tasks

## Grooming Steps
- Review highest‑priority items first
- Clarify acceptance criteria
- Add missing details (dependencies, risks, assumptions)
- Re‑estimate items if needed
- Merge duplicates; remove obsolete items
- Reorder based on value, urgency, and dependencies

## What “Ready” Means
- Clear description
- Acceptance criteria defined
- Dependencies known
- Estimated (points or hours)
- Small enough to complete within a sprint

# Backlog Grooming Thinking Guide

- Does each item have a clear user or business value?
- Are we grooming often enough to avoid sprint planning chaos?
- Which items are too large and need splitting?
- Are we carrying items that will never be prioritized?
- Are stakeholders aligned on priority?
- What assumptions need validation before work begins?
