# Definition of Ready (DoR) and Definition of Done (DoD)

Ensure work enters the sprint in a clear, actionable state (Ready) and exits the sprint meeting the team’s agreed‑upon standards (Done). They reduce rework, prevent ambiguity, and support predictable delivery.

---

## Definition of Ready (DoR)

### DoR Checklist
- Clear description of the work
- Acceptance criteria defined
- Dependencies identified
- Assumptions documented
- Estimated (points or hours)
- Small enough to complete within a sprint
- No external blockers
- Stakeholder alignment confirmed (if needed)

### DoR Thinking Guide
- Does the team understand the work well enough to start immediately?
- Are acceptance criteria testable and unambiguous?
- Is the item too large and in need of splitting?
- Are we carrying items that will never be prioritized?
- What assumptions need validation before work begins?
- Does this item align with the sprint goal or upcoming priorities?

---

## Definition of Done (DoD)

### DoD Checklist
- Work meets acceptance criteria
- Code/content reviewed (if applicable)
- Tests passed or validation completed
- Documentation updated (if required)
- No unresolved defects or blockers
- Work deployed, delivered, or integrated
- Stakeholder acceptance (if required)
- Meets team’s quality standards

### DoD Thinking Guide
- What does “complete” mean for this team or project?
- Are quality standards consistent across all work items?
- What validation steps are mandatory before marking an item Done?
- Does Done include deployment, or only readiness for deployment?
- Are we unintentionally accepting “almost done” work?
