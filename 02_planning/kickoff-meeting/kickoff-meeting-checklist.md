# Kickoff Meeting Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure the kickoff meeting is prepared, executed, and followed up with clarity and alignment. This checklist includes pre‑kickoff preparation, meeting execution, and post‑meeting actions.

---

# 1. Pre‑Kickoff Preparation (Before Scheduling the Meeting)

### Project Foundations
- [ ] Project Charter is complete and approved  
- [ ] Stakeholder Register is complete  
- [ ] Communication Plan is drafted  
- [ ] RACI Matrix is finalized  
- [ ] Project goals, scope, and boundaries are clearly defined  
- [ ] Key milestones and target launch date are identified  

### Team Readiness
- [ ] Core team roles are confirmed  
- [ ] Sponsor availability is validated  
- [ ] SMEs and cross‑functional partners identified  
- [ ] Any known risks or constraints documented  

### Logistics
- [ ] Determine whether kickoff is virtual, hybrid, or in‑person. Based on our stakeholders, budget, and timeline, what is most realistic?   
- [ ] Confirm time zones for all attendees  
- [ ] Validate that a 1‑hour meeting is appropriate. If not, adjust the plan accordingly.   
- [ ] Select meeting platform (Google Meet, Teams, Zoom, etc.)  
- [ ] Confirm recording permissions (if applicable)  

---

# 2. Before the Meeting (After Scheduling)

### Agenda & Materials
- [ ] Kickoff agenda created  
- [ ] Agenda shared with attendees  
- [ ] Pre‑reads sent (Project Charter, RACI, Communication Plan, etc.)  
- [ ] Speakers, as needed, identified for each agenda section  
- [ ] Meeting goals clearly stated in the invite
- [ ] Company culture considered and applied.  (i.e. refereshments, other meeting norms) 

### Tools & Setup
- [ ] Notetaker assigned  
- [ ] Recording enabled (if appropriate)  
- [ ] Screen‑sharing tested  
- [ ] Slides or visual aids prepared (optional)  
- [ ] Confidence poll prepared (1–5 scale)
- [ ] Team communication and task tools, if used, tested. Stakeholder acounts & permissions setup & tested. 

### Attendee Alignment
- [ ] All RACI roles invited  
- [ ] Sponsor confirmed  
- [ ] External partners invited (if applicable)  
- [ ] Confirm no critical stakeholders are missing  

---

# 3. During the Meeting

### Introductions
- [ ] SMILE: bring your best enthusism and welcoming tone/exicitment 
- [ ] Team introductions completed  
- [ ] Roles clarified  
- [ ] Optional: 1 fun fact for team building 

### Project Background
- [ ] Why the project exists explained  
- [ ] Why it matters to the organization clarified  

### Goals & Scope
- [ ] Project goals reviewed  
- [ ] In‑scope items explained  
- [ ] Out‑of‑scope items reinforced  
- [ ] Target launch date shared  
- [ ] Key milestones highlighted  

### Roles & Responsibilities
- [ ] RACI roles summarized  
- [ ] Accountability clarified  
- [ ] Expectations set for each role  

### Collaboration & Communication
- [ ] Tools reviewed (email, chat, docs, issue tracking)  
- [ ] Meeting cadence shared  
- [ ] Async vs. sync expectations clarified  
- [ ] Accessibility considerations discussed  
- [ ] Decision‑making pathways explained  

### What’s Next
- [ ] Immediate next steps shared  
- [ ] Early deliverables assigned  
- [ ] Timeline for first working session confirmed
- [ ] Agreement on next steps 

### Q&A + Confidence Poll
- [ ] Open questions addressed  
- [ ] Confidence poll completed (1–5)  
- [ ] If anyone <4, concerns documented  
- [ ] Roadblocks identified  

---

# 4. After the Meeting

### Follow‑Up Communication
- [ ] Summary email sent  
- [ ] Meeting notes and/or slide deck shared  
- [ ] Recording link shared (if applicable)  
- [ ] Action items documented and assigned  
- [ ] Links to all project documents included  

### Alignment & Adjustments
- [ ] Address concerns from confidence poll  
- [ ] Clarify any ambiguous responsibilities  
- [ ] Update documents (Charter, RACI, Communication Plan) as needed  
- [ ] Confirm next meeting or working session  

---

# Final Review
- [ ] All key stakeholders attended or received follow‑up  
- [ ] Team is aligned on goals, scope, and roles  
- [ ] Collaboration norms are understood  
- [ ] Action items are assigned with owners and due dates  
- [ ] Confidence poll concerns addressed  
- [ ] Kickoff meeting supports a smooth transition into execution
