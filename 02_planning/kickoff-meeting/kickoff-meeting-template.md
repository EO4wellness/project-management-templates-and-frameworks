# 4. Sample Agenda (Approximately 1 Hour)

| Time | Topic | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 10 min | **Introductions** | Name, project role, 1 fun fact per person |
| 5 min | **Project Background** | Why this project exists and why it matters |
| 5 min | **Goals & Scope** | In‑scope, out‑of‑scope, target launch date, key milestones |
| 5 min | **Roles & Responsibilities** | Who is accountable, who is responsible, who is consulted/informed |
| 10 min | **Collaboration & Communication** | Tools, expectations, async/sync norms, meeting cadence |
| 10 min | **What’s Next** | Immediate next steps, expectations, early deliverables |
| 15 min | **Q&A + Confidence Poll** | Open questions + 1–5 confidence rating, remove blockers |

# 10. Confidence Poll
Ask each attendee to rate their confidence in the project from **1 (not confident)** to **5 (very confident)**.

**If anyone rates below 4:**  
Document what the PM can do to increase confidence and remove early roadblocks.

---

# 11. Notes
Capture key discussion points, decisions, risks, or concerns raised during the meeting. As the project manager typically presents the Kick Off Meeting, an assistant should be assigned to take notes and/or the meeting should be recorded. 

---

# 12. Follow‑Up
After the meeting, the PM should:

- Send a summary email  
- Share meeting notes  
- Confirm action items  
- Provide links to all project documents  
- Ask for additional questions or clarifications  
- Clear up any lack-of-confidence or other blockers
