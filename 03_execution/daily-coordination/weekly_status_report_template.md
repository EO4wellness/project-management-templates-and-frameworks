# Weekly Status Report  
Weekly status reports provide a structured, predictable way to communicate project progress, risks, changes, and upcoming priorities. They help stakeholders stay aligned, reduce surprises, and ensure the project remains on track against scope, schedule, and budget.

This toolkit includes a **Template**, a **Checklist**, a **Thinking Guide**, and a **Role‑Based Example** to demonstrate how weekly status reporting supports transparency and effective decision‑making.

## Weekly Status Checklist

- Summarize progress clearly and concisely  
- Use role‑based ownership for clarity  
- Highlight blockers early  
- Update risks and issues with current status  
- Confirm dependencies are accurate  
- Identify decisions needed from leadership  
- Note any changes to scope, budget, or timeline  
- Keep the tone neutral, factual, and blameless  
- Include visuals (Gantt, roadmap, burndown) when helpful  
- Optionally use AI tools to generate summaries or detect patterns

## Weekly Status Thinking Guide

**Accomplishments**  
- What moved the project forward?  
- Did we hit any milestones?  
- Did any deliverables reach Definition of Done?  

**Priorities**  
- What must be completed next week to stay on schedule?  
- Are priorities shifting due to risks or changes?  

**Risks & Issues**  
- What new risks emerged?  
- Did any risks escalate in severity?  
- Are issues trending or repeating?  
- Does anything require escalation?  

**Dependencies**  
- Are upstream teams on track?  
- Are downstream teams waiting on us?  
- Did any external vendors slip?  

**Changes**  
- Did scope, budget, or timeline shift?  
- Do we need a Change Request?  

**Decisions Needed**  
- What decisions are blocking progress?  
- Who needs to make them?  
- When are they required?
